Undergraduate Research

BCOE is committed to providing undergraduate students an experience that will best prepare them for a career in engineering. Hands-on research opportunities with faculty help students develop real-world technical skills while participating in collaborative work environments common in the field. 

Few other top-tier research institutions can match our commitment to student research opportunities. BCOE has more than $30 million in sponsored research each year and undergraduates have real opportunities to participate in and contribute to the innovative work done in these laboratories to further enrich the educational experience and prepare for life after graduation.

Team NOx-Out Wins Odebrecht Award for Sustainable Development

BCOE's NOx-Out team placed first in the Odebrecht Award for Sustainable Development competition for its development of a lawn mower attachment that reduces air pollution. The team, led by Dr. Kwai Tam and researchers Alyssa Yan, Priyanka Singh, and Anna Almario, competed nationally to be named a top three finalist and was awarded first place ahead of Duke University and the University of California, Berkeley.

The project, titled "NOx-Out: Selective Catalytic Reduction System for Emissions Control of Small Off-Road Engines," focused on teh development of a device that drastically reduces air pollutant emissions from lawn mowers. Unlike automobiles, most lawn mowers do not contain catalytic converters, which transform air pollutants to less harmful emissions.

A test run at BCOE found that the device could reduce carbon monoxide by 87 percent and nitrogen oxides by 67 percent. More recent improvements have increased the capture of particulate matter from 44 percent to 93 percent.

Husk-to-Home Team Wins EPA P3 Phase II Award

Colin Eckerle, Jose Corbala-Delgado, Brandon Leu, Kevin Li, Jacqueline Ortega, Brian Rojas-Lerena, Dennis Jones
Chemical and Environmental  Engineering & Business Administration

BCOE's Husk-to-Home team has been awarded a $75,000 grant from the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) to build housing in the Phillipines using a termite-resistant composite material created from waste rice husks. The product, which the students believe could be used for furniture in the United States, will last longer, be environmentally friendly, and cost less when compared to comparable alternatives.

New App Connects Students and Tutors

Sultan Khan and Haasith Sanka
Computer Science and Engineering

At a recent hackathon, two undergraduate computer science students created Scholarly, an on-demand tutoring service that connects students with nearby tutors. The app earned them a first place win at the world's largest education hackathon, HackingEDU. Described as the "Uber for Tutors," Scholarly users can view tutor profiles, set meeting locations and get help with their studies with a click of a button.
