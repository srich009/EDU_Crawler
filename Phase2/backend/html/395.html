Freshman: Fall

Locate the Career Center.

  • Book market the campus map.

Get the quarterly career calendar.

  • Our quarterly career calendar lists workshops, career fairs, employer information sessions and more!
  • Attend the Freshman Game Plan Workshop.

Log in to SCOTLink.

  • Find job listings, build your resume, research options and more by clicking here.

Learn about resources.

  • Visit faculty during office hours.
  • Go to the Academic Resource Center (ARC).
  • Visit the Counseling Center.

Freshman: Winter


 Draft resume.

  • Attend a Resume Writing Workshop and/or use the Resume Builder in SCOTLink.
  • Learn more about resumes and see examples.

Get involved! Consider joining a business-related student organization.

  • Meet new people and build skills by joining a student organization or volunteering.

Research summer opportunities.

  • Summer school, part- time jobs, and/or volunteering are all options. Check out SCOTJobs and these other websites for more information:
    • CareerShift
    • HandsOn Inland Empire
    • Snag a Job
    • UCR Summer Session

 

Freshman: Spring


Resume critiqued.

  • Get your resume reviewed during drop-in hours at the Career Center (Monday-Thursday, 10 a.m.-3 p.m., and Friday, 10 a.m.-noon).

Explore academic majors and meet with a pre-business advisor.

  • Review UCR majors, read the upper division course descriptions in the course catalog, and talk to upper division students and/or academic advisors in preferred majors.

Research summer opportunities and internships.

  • Summer school, part- time jobs, internships and/or volunteering are all options. Check out SCOTJobs and these other websites for more information:
    • CareerShift
    • HandsOn Inland Empire
    • Snag a Job
    • UCR Summer Session

Freshman: Summer


Summer experience.

  • Check out The Back Door Guide to Short-Term Job Adventures by Michael Landes for ideas.

Informational interviews.

  • Meet with professionals in your field of interest and ask questions about their jobs. Learn more about informational interviews.

Set goals.

  • What skills would you like to learn? Any professionals you’d like to meet? Make an action plan! Learn more about setting goals.

Sophomore: Fall


Attend the Sophomore Game Plan Workshop.

Update resume.

  • College experiences should start to take the place of high school experiences on your resume.

Explore majors/careers.

  • Meet with a Career Counselor to discuss how to connect your major choice with your career options.  Check out our Career Planning section.  
  • Keep an eye out for our Job Discovery Series: Careers in Your Major. You can explore careers and network with business professionals.
  • Use these online resources:
    • CareerInsider (via SCOTLink)
    • Focus 2 (via SCOTLink)
    • O*NET 
    • Career-Planning Tips from Quintcareers.com

Internships Research.

  • Great way to try out a company or career. Attend an internship workshop and explore internship listings.

Stay involved!

For ideas, check out student organizations and volunteering opportunities.

Sophomore: Winter


Apply for internships.

  • Many structured, summer internship programs have application deadlines in the winter quarter. Plan ahead! Explore the links below for opportunities and deadline details.
    • SCOTJobs 
    • CareerShift (via SCOTLink)
    • Inroads.org
    • SEO (Sponsors of Educational Opportunities)
    • HACU (Hispanic Association of Colleges & Universities) National Internship Program

Try leadership.

  • Volunteer to coordinate a project or committee.

Practice interviewing.

  • Attend an Interview Skills Workshop, and practice with the Mock Interview Tool and make an appointment with a counselor to go over answers.

Sophomore: Spring


Apply for internships.

  • Internship positions are still available. Check out opportunities in SCOTJobs.

Apply to the Business Administration major.

Practice networking.

  • Attend a Connecting Your Way to A Job or Social Media in Your Job Search Workshop.
  • Stay active with your LinkedIn account and join groups and organizations related to your field of interest.

Attend the Last Chance Career Fair.

  • Employers will be on campus to discuss full-time, part-time and internship openings at the Last Chance Career Fair. Learn more.

 

Sophomore: Summer


Summer experience.

  • Capitalize on opportunities, paid or unpaid that will help you explore fields of interest.

Informational interviews.

  • Meet with professionals in your field of interest and ask questions about their jobs. Learn more about informational interviews.

Review & re-set goals.

  • Reflect and identify next steps.

Junior: Fall


Attend the Junior Game Plan Workshop.

Update resume.

  • Highlight related experience. No more high school info.

Research internships (summer internships could lead to a potential job offer after graduation)!

  • For academic credit, make sure to register for the 198I course in your major.
  • Use all of your resources (i.e. SCOTLink, career counselors, friends, family and professional associations) to target relevant internships.

Discuss career options.

  • Make an appointment with career counselor to discuss/identify your options.

Practice interview skills.

  • Attend an Interview Skills Workshop.
  • Use the Mock Interview tool in SCOTLink.

Attend career fairs on campus.

  • Attend the Finance, Accounting, and Business Fair.
  • See our calendar.


MBA? MPAc? Law school? Graduate school? If you are considering graduate school, discuss your options with an advisor/counselor.

  • If interested, the UCR Academic Resource Center offers exam prep seminars. You can also check out Kaplan and Princeton Review.

Junior: Winter


Apply for internships.

  • Browse through listings on SCOTJobs. Don’t forget the directories in our Resource Room!

Network!

  • The top skill employers search for is communication. Practice public speaking and basic conversational skills.  Learn more about networking.

Target career options.

  • How can you locate opportunities in a specific industry or location? Attend a Where Are the Jobs Workshop to find out.

Junior: Spring

Apply for internships.

  • Didn’t find anything on SCOTJobs? Try contacting companies directly. Check out Hoovers and the Book of Lists published by the business press for each county.

Discover On-Campus Interview (OCI) Program.

  • Employers interview UCR students at the Career Center for internship and full-time positions. Learn more.

Attend the Career Night Job Fair.

  • Practice introducing and marketing yourself to employers and attend our Career Night Job Fair.
  • Research participating companies.

Attend the Last Chance Job Fair.

  • Practice introducing and marketing yourself to employers and attend our Last Chance Job Fair.
  • Research participating companies.
  • Attend our Connecting Your Way to a Job Workshop.

If you plan to apply to graduate school, you should take your GMAT, GRE, LSAT, etc. at the end of your junior year or beginning of your senior year.

  • For MPAc, make sure you fulfill CPA requirements.
  • Collect reference letters if you are considering grad school.

Junior: Summer


Gain experience.

  • Be curious about the job opportunities in your environment. What are the different positions available? What are the qualifications for those jobs? Try to get a full-time summer internship if possible.

Network

  • Identify five professionals who can mentor you through the full-time job search next year. Learn more about the value of having mentors.

Review and set goals.

  • Reflect on what you’ve experienced so far and identify next steps.

Senior:Fall


Attend the Senior Game Plan Workshop.

Develop a targeted resume.

  • Tailor your resume to the company or industry you want to work for. Attend a Resume Writing Workshop and meet with a career counselor.

Participate in the On Campus Interview (OCI) Program.

  • By interviewing with employers through OCI you are drastically reducing the competition for jobs. Learn more.

Practice interview skills.

  • Log in to SCOTLink and use the Mock Interview Tool.

If you plan to go to graduate school right after graduation, make sure you have taken the appropriate test (GMAT, GRE, LSAT, etc.) and start applying for admission.

Senior: Winter


NETWORK! (start junior year)

  • Keep your contacts updated on your job search. Ask for advice and guidance regarding openings…NOT A JOB!
  • Learn more about networking.

Apply/interview for positions.

  • Remember to follow up with employers to make sure they received your resume and/or application.

Senior: Spring


Participate in On-Campus Interviews (OCI).

  • Many employers interview students at the Career Center for full-time positions. Learn more.

Attend Career Night and the Last Chance Job Fair.

  • This is your time to shine! Practice your intro to employers, take plenty of copies of your resume and definitely research the participating employers BEFORE the event. Learn more.

Tell us your plans!

  • Complete your placement information in SCOTLink so that we can tell administrators, the employer community and future UCR students about your accomplishments! Go to "profile," then "placements."

 

Senior: Summer


Begin your exciting career!

  • We will continue to support you even after you graduate! Take advantage of our alumni services.
