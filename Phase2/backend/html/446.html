GradSuccess

GradSuccess provides a variety of services to meet the needs of UCRâ€™s diverse graduate student population. Housed in Graduate Division, GradSuccess offers programs, workshops, seminars, and consultations by appointment and drop-in. GradSuccess supports graduate students at every stage of their study and is concerned with helping students become successful professionals.

 GradSuccess is comprised of:

  • Graduate Student Resource Center
  • Teaching Assistant Development Program
    • University Teaching Certificate program
    • International TA Program
  • Mentoring Program 
  • Graduate Writing Center
  • GradQuant

 

Graduate Student Resource Center

The Graduate Student Resource Center offers a diverse array of programming in conjunction with campus partners. In the area of personal development, the center offers stress relief events, opportunities for students to meet and network with each other, and financial wellness workshops. The center organizes full-day academic and professional development symposiums quarterly. In the Fall, Academic Development Day includes workshops and seminars to help students succeed in their graduate student careers and prepare of future academic careers. In the Winter, Beyond Academia Day has workshops and seminars to help graduate student prepare for non-academic careers. Beyond Academia Day also includes a networking event. In the Spring, Teaching Development Day has now expanded into a week of programming. It includes a full-day of seminars focused on university level pedagogy, a recruitment day with liberal arts college faculty, and panels with community college and Cal State University faculty. Throughout the year, the center also offers smaller events to help students achieve success in their personal, professional, and academic lives.

Graduate Student Resource Center

 

Teaching Assistant Development Program

The Teaching Assistant Development Program trains all TAs at UCR who are at varying levels of their instructional career.  First-time TAs attend an orientation and continuing TAs are encouraged to attend TA Enrichment seminars hosted in conjunction with the resource center.  TAs who have not achieved satisfactory evaluations receive individual mentoring.  Those who have excelled are eligible for the Outstanding Teaching Assistant Award and Distinguished Teaching Award for Graduate Students offered through Graduate Division.  For those who seek advanced pedagogical training in university-level instruction, the TA Development Program offers the University Teaching Certificate program.  Additionally, TADP provides resources to support UCR's large community of international teaching assistants.                                                              
Teaching Assistant Development Program

 

Mentorship Program

The Graduate Student Mentoring Program offers incoming graduate students the opportunity to connect with both a peer and faculty mentor.  The program is unique as it pairs each peer mentor with several incoming students and each faculty mentor with two peer mentors, creating a mentoring tree.  Incoming students attend an extended 3-day orientation prior to the beginning of school. Throughout the year students meet with their peer and faculty mentors both individually and in groups in order to develop a network of mentoring and to foster a sense of community.  
Mentorship Program

 

Graduate Writing Center

The Graduate Writing Center offers all graduate students support in developing their work in the many writing genres required by academic disciplines and departments.  Individual consultations are available by appointment through the website.  The Graduate Writing Center also offers workshops and seminars in conjunction with the Resource Center which present broader topics such as publishing practices and drafting appropriate job search materials.                        
Graduate Writing Center

 

GradQuant

In the Winter of 2014, GradSuccess launched the new Graduate Quantitative Methods Center (GradQuant).  The center offers training in probability and statistical inference, statistical software and computing, math for statistics, data management, professional ethics in the management and analysis of data, and digital humanities software.  The educational support includes seminars and workshops in introductory methods; advanced, specialized topics workshops; and individual consultations.
GradQuant

 

Partner Programs

GradSuccess is proud to partner with The Well, the Career Center, the Counseling and Psychological Services, the Veterans Service Center, the Student Disability Resource Center, UCR Libraries, and the International Students and Scholars Program to provide many of our programs for our students.
